# Anti-AI Writing Rules

Use these rules for drafts before anything is added to the public site.

## Core Standard

Write like a person with a specific point of view, not like a model trying to sound polished. Every paragraph should earn its place by saying something concrete, useful, or emotionally true.

## Do

- Use plain words first.
- Make one clear point per paragraph.
- Prefer specific examples over broad claims.
- Keep sentences varied, but natural.
- Use direct verbs instead of inflated phrasing.
- Let strong ideas stay simple.
- Include lived details when they are true and relevant.
- Cut any sentence that only repeats the headline.
- Use contractions when they sound natural.
- Read the draft aloud before publishing.

## Avoid

- Avoid generic openings like "In today's world" or "It is important to note."
- Avoid filler transitions like "Furthermore," "Moreover," and "In conclusion."
- Avoid motivational-poster language.
- Avoid over-explaining obvious points.
- Avoid fake balance when the article has a clear stance.
- Avoid repeating the same structure in every paragraph.
- Avoid vague intensifiers like "very," "really," "incredibly," and "significantly" unless they add meaning.
- Avoid phrases that sound assembled from common internet advice.
- Avoid sterile summaries at the end.
- Avoid making every paragraph the same length.

## Banned Phrases

Do not use these unless quoting someone:

- "delve into"
- "unlock your potential"
- "game changer"
- "journey toward"
- "in the realm of"
- "navigate the complexities"
- "a testament to"
- "it is crucial to"
- "serves as a reminder"
- "more than just"
- "not only... but also"
- "ever-evolving"
- "holistic approach"
- "leverage"
- "empower"
- "seamlessly"
- "robust"
- "transformative"

## Revision Checklist

Before publishing, check the draft against these questions:

1. Does the first sentence say something specific?
2. Could any paragraph appear on any other self-improvement site?
3. Are there concrete details, examples, or observations?
4. Did I remove filler transitions?
5. Did I remove generic advice that does not help the reader act?
6. Does the ending leave the reader with a useful thought instead of a bland recap?

## Tone For NoArousal

- Direct, calm, and grounded.
- Honest about discomfort and setbacks.
- Practical before inspirational.
- Serious without being dramatic.
- Human, not clinical.

## Final Pass

If a sentence sounds like it was written to impress the internet, rewrite it until it sounds like something a real person would say to another real person.
